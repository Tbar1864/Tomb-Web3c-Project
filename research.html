It explores the role of HTML as the backbone of web content and details the history and foundational technologies of the World Wide Web (WWW). Invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 at CERN, the WWW emerged to address the need for global information-sharing. The report also highlights the significance of TCP/IP, developed in the 1970s, as the protocol enabling global computer communication. The growth of the WWW was marked by the creation of search engines, starting with Archie in 1990, and followed by Yahoo, AltaVista, and Google, which revolutionized information retrieval. Overall, search engine technology played a crucial role in transforming the WWW into an essential global resource, influencing numerous industries and shaping how people access and share information today."
